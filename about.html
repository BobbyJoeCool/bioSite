About Josh

Josh’s interest in blacksmithing began as a quiet fascination rather than a sudden decision. From a young age, he was drawn to creating useful objects rather than simply purchasing them. From an interest in times before manufacturing, where everything was crafted by hand, to fantasy worlds where entire civilizations revolved around the forge, Josh always felt drawn to the flame of molten steel. That opportunity to try for himself came when he happened across a cheap anvil and took the chance and purchased it. Slowly, he built a small, humble forge in his backyard, where he spends a lot of his free time, twisting the metal into the shapes he wants.

Josh gravitates toward forging practical household items, valuing usefulness over display. For him, the most rewarding part of the craft is seeing something he made become part of everyday life. A typical day in the forge follows a steady routine: cleaning out the firepot, restocking coal, and lighting the forge. While the fire comes up to temperature, he decides whether to continue an existing project or begin something new, letting the rhythm of preparation set the pace for the work ahead.

He enjoys nearly every stage of the blacksmithing process, from shaping hot steel to refining final details. The greatest challenges arise when he pushes himself to try new techniques, especially when making knives, which require careful planning and attention to detail. Through blacksmithing, Josh has learned the value of patience and the importance of slowing down, as rushing often leads to mistakes. The craft has also deepened his appreciation for handmade metalwork, while making him more discerning about quality and pricing.

Josh was diagnosed with Asperger’s Syndrome, which is now recognized to be a part of the autism spectrum umbrella, and that influences how he approaches the craft. The repetition and focus required in forging can be grounding, and he tends to research techniques deeply rather than rely solely on imitation. Autism can cause a person to fixate on details and dive deeply into a single topic. Looking ahead, he has no fixed expectations but hopes to continue improving and eventually sell more of the pieces he creates.
